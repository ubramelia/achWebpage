    • Nancy Drew and the Mystery of the Bad Acting

    Every good mystery starts with introducing the characters and setting the scene. So close your eyes; Imagine a chubby little girl who watches too much Glee and dresses like Ashley Tisdale circa 2000. If you're picturing a hot mess, you're right on the money because that little girl was me. One day I was at one of those city events where they do raffles and sell $5 sodas and $15 funnel cakes when I see a stand playing 'I'll Make a Man Out of You' from Mulan while a guy with a stick danced in front of it. I was visibly intrigued so a girl walked up, handed me a flyer, and asked if I wanted to audition for a production of Mulan: The jr. Broadway Musical with a community theatre company called Debbie's Angels Productions. I said yes and few months later I was badly performing onstage a song I never learned the notes to as Grandmother Fa while I nevously sweated through my stage makeup (more on that later). From that moment on I was *shiver* A THEATRE KID.

    After that, I was hooked and proceded to perform in the next few shows Debbie's Angels put together including The Wizard of Oz as Aunt Em and Beauty and the Beast as Babette. I even did an Onslow County Schools production of High School Musical as Random Nerd. During the DAP shows, I made friends with a few older kids and together we also did an adult (or mostly adult) production of Romeo and Juliet in which I played Romeo's mother and the chick who gave him the poison to kill himself with (respectivley; To be clear his mother did not give him the poison, that would be super fucked up and not very motherly). It was even set in a dark carnival theme so I got to dress up as a gypsy. But that was when the story turned dark... Just kidding, it didn't, and this story isn't really even a mystery, actually so I totally misled you but a girl's gotta do what a girl's gotta do to make people listen to (or in this case, read) her life story, especially one this boring. The tale's not over though so read on to hear about how I think I'm way smarter than I actually am.



    Once a Standardized Test Told me I was Smart and I've Been Annoying Ever Since

    You got a glimpse of who I was as a 6th grader in the last section; A "chubby little girl who watches too much Glee and dresses like Ashley Tisdalecirca 2000," in the words of myself. So how did I ever get the confidence to rock that look? Well, as the title suggests, once a standardized test told me I was smart and I've been annoying ever since. Actually, it was more like a collection of pointless tests with basically meaningless results that had no practical ramifications oncesoever for most people but, the point is, those meaningless results meant a lot to me. Ever since kindergarden, I have been told I was gifted. I was always in the highest 'reading level' and in the group that got to leave the class and work ahead of the others because we were just 'so much smarter', we were the royalty of the academic pyramid so, naturally, it would be a sin to hold us down to the peasants' level of learning. Now every kid wants to feel special but imagine every teacher actually telling you that from a young age, that you were destined for success. You'd start believing it; Spoiler Alert: I did.


    It got worse when I got into 3rd grade and there were actual tests to tell you where you stood in comparison to the others. The EOGs or End of Grade standardized tests were my proof that I was better than everyone else. The other students despised them for giving them a responsibility to study and the anxiety of a single test telling them whether or not they should be allowed to pass. I, however, LOVED them. I didn't need to study, I never even learned how to and I was already told I was smart so I had no worry, only excitement. When I got my test results, they reaffirmed my suspicions that I was a Geniusâ„¢. I scored higher than almost everyone in the school and was immediatley thrown into the higher level classes all the way through elementary school. In fact, I was the first student in the school to be certified as an AIG (Academically and Intellectually Gifted) student which means, in fifth grade, I was taken out of class an hour and a half a day to study one-on-one higher level material (Pre-algebra and Latin) with a new teacher. When I got to 6th grade, I was put in a class ahead of the others in which we learned our regular math and Pre-Algebra. Now, if you were paying attention, you'll remember that I already learned Pre-algebra. So now I'm in this class of smart kids and I already know the material so I'm seen as the smartest of the already smart class. Yes, my ego was affected accordingly. But here comes the best part; I was asked to join a program called the Duke University TIP (Talent Identification Program). That program involved taking the SATs in 7th grade. So when I scored well on that, I was ready to call up Steven Hawkings and tell him I was definitley his daughter because there was no way my genius brain came from my family, it just wasn't possible. That's the end of this chapter but if you had fun reading about how my self-esteem grew 18 sizes too big, you're gonna LOVE learning how it all toppled down and I realized just how insignificant and average I am in the sequel. Keep reading!



    My Time Making People's Faces look like Other Faces

    If you were waiting to here how my self-esteem dropped like thee sickest of all beats, you'll have to wait a little longer because this is the story of how I became the almost decent makeup artist I am to this day. Remember when I said I did a production of Mulan? Well, during that production we had a day when we all got to take headshots. If you don't know, headshots are basically pictures of your face and neck that you give to directors and casting agents so that they can judge you to see if they want you in their movie or play or whatever. You want to look your best for those so DAP had a proffessional make-up artist come in and make us look presentable. It was on that day that I fell in love with makeup. Now, don't get me wrong, I was not an attractive girl so, even with the makeup, I didn't look great, but it was such a huge step up from what I normally looked like, I swooned. I swooned over the makeup God who made my face look decent, I swooned over the makeup she used to do it, and I swooned over the compliments I got from everyone once it was on. From that day on I spent years doing shitty makeup through a trial and lots of error process to get to where I am now. By the time I was doing Romeo and Juliet, I was good enough that they let me do my own and even some of the other actors' makeup. After succeeding there, I continued as a makeup artist at school where I charged people $5 to get their makeup done for the day and I did some of my friends' prom makeup. Then, after seeing my makeup skills get better, DAP let me be a makeup intern for their next Jr. Broadway show, Annie. I did the makeup for a bunch of kids and other teens for 5 shows. Now I mostly do my own makeup for going out and such but hopefully, later this year, I will be doing their makeup again for Aladdin Jr..



    Once a Standardized Test Told me I was Smart and I've Been Annoying Ever Since (THE SEQUEL)

    Now the moment we've all been waiting for, my downfall. That's a bit dramatic but, hey, I'm an actor. So remember how all my teachers told me I was destined for greatness? Turns out, those aren't just empty complimets. Those suckers are filled with high expectations. Once you start getting those, you can't go back. From now on, you're the kid in the 'you're gonna go far kid' sayings. You don't have another choice; If you're not a millionare by 40 then you aren't using your 'gift'. Here's the kicker though- YOU NEVER HAD A GIFT. And by you, I mean me. That's right, I'm just an average girl with no outstanding talents or skills. And I know you're thinking "But, Amelia, what about those tests that proved you were super smart?", well they fucking lied. I learned that standardized tests are mostly bullshit and a lot of the people I looked down on for not scoring well were just bad test-takers. I was only marginally smarter in kindergarden through 2nd grade and there's no proof that I was anything above average after that. That's all fine and I realized it in 8th grade. I took a blow to the self-esteem but, honestly, I needed that to force me to wear some decent clothes. In high school, however, it got much worse. Remember when I said I never learned to study because I was able to barely pay attention in class and still ace everything? It turns out that once you get into the higher math classes, that doesn't cut it anymore. I suddenly got to a point where paying attention and studying was necassary. Except, I was never trained for that. Well, maybe I was and I just wasn't paying attention because I thought I was the exception to everything. Freshman year I dropped out of Math 2 and barely passed some of my other classes. I suddenly realized I am no one special, an insignificant human on one little planet in a galaxy that's barely out of it's diapers. So I figured that the most important thing in life shouldn't be just to succeed. It should be to be happy and hopefully work my way up to that same feeling I used to have of self-importance, but actually earn it this time. Everyone finds happiness in their own way but I know that to me, money equals happiness. Keep reading to see how I plan on getting it.



    I Need Lots of Money to Fuel my Narcassistic Thoughts and Here's How I'm Gonna Get It

    If you've been following along, I went from being an overconfident dilusionalist with an inflated sense of self-importance to being self-aware and quite possibly in a mid-life crisis which, if i'm lucky, means I'm half-way to an early death. I had that realization that the only way to come back from here is to be well-educated and make a lot of money. Easier said than done, I know, but I have a plan. Computer programming. It's the best career path I can think of. I have other passions, of course. I love performing but I lack the talent to make risking a career in the arts worthwile. I enjoy doing makeup but even as a professional makeup artist, I'll never make the money I really want to. I find anything medical fascinating and I'd love to be a doctor except it would take nearly 10 years of school; Not to mention I have depression and sleep issues so working those extremely long shifts with no breaks is NOT a possibility and with my severe general anxiety disorder, nobody should put their life in my hands. Plus, I'm REALLY lazy. The only career I know that I can almost assure my own success in while giving me decent working hours with great pay and little risk is computer programming.


    For starters, it's one of the best paying jobs right now. I also have 2 brothers who are both programmers so that's basically like having a foot in the door. I'm more likely to get accepted to their schools and get jobs/internships at IBM (since they both have worked/work there). So, boiled down, that's why I'm making this website. It's a practice website for me to build my skills until I have something to be proud of. When I'm done with this, I'll make a proffessional portfolio that I can put on my resume. Hopefully, it won't be as dramatic and it will look a lot better. Until then, thanks for reading!
